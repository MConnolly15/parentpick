Sudocrem vs Metanium vs Bepanthen 2026

We used all three across two kids in Belfast. Here's what actually matters.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Real-world testing • Prices checked April 2026

All three are sold in every UK pharmacy, all claim to treat nappy rash, none are prescribed by NHS as "best" – because they all work as barriers. The difference is when you use them, how they feel, and whether your baby's skin tolerates them. We spent about £45 total testing these over 18 months.

Sudocrem – the UK default

It's in 90% of UK changing bags for a reason. Works, cheap, available everywhere including Tesco at 11pm. The zinc oxide creates good barrier, plus it has mild antiseptic which helps if skin is slightly broken. Downsides: the fragrance and lanolin irritated our second child's eczema-prone skin. Also stains clothes yellow if you use too much. For daily prevention on normal skin, it's hard to beat for £3.75.

How we used it: pea-sized, every change from 2 weeks to 6 months. No rash for 4 months straight.

Metanium – the heavy duty option

Pharmacist recommended this when our first got proper weaning rash at 9 months – bright red, sore, crying at changes. It's much thicker, almost like Vaseline with grit. Creates a serious waterproof layer. The titanium dioxide is more occlusive than zinc. It worked in 36 hours where Sudocrem hadn't shifted it in 3 days. But it's overkill for daily use – too thick, hard to spread, and at £5.50 for tiny 30g tube, expensive if used daily. We kept it as "emergency" cream.

Key tip: warm it between fingers first, then apply ultra-thin. If you glob it on, it cakes and makes rash worse.

Bepanthen – the gentle one

Switched to this at 7 months when we realised Sudocrem was aggravating eczema patches on elbows (lanolin sensitivity). Bepanthen has no fragrance, no lanolin, just dexpanthenol which helps skin repair. Texture is like smooth ointment, spreads easily, doesn't leave white cast. It's not antiseptic, purely barrier and soothing. For sensitive skin, it's worth the extra £2.50. Lasts longer too – 100g tube lasted us 10 weeks vs 6 weeks for Sudocrem.

NHS health visitors often recommend Bepanthen for newborns because it's so gentle.

What NHS actually says

NHS doesn't recommend brands. Their guidance: "use a thin layer of barrier cream containing zinc and castor oil or petroleum jelly." All three meet this. The choice is personal preference and skin tolerance. See our full NHS application guide.

Real world verdict

If budget is tight: Sudocrem for everything. If baby has active, sore rash: buy Metanium for 3 days, then back to Sudocrem. If baby has eczema, sensitive skin, or you hate fragrance: Bepanthen. We ended up with Bepanthen daily and Metanium in cupboard for emergencies – total cost about £12 every 3 months.

Cheaper alternatives that work

Asda Little Angels Zinc & Castor Oil Cream £1.75 – same zinc concentration as Sudocrem, no fragrance. Tesco own brand £2. Works identically. Don't pay for brand if money is tight.
