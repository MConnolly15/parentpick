White Noise: How Loud Is Safe?

Use it safely, and know when to stop.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

White noise can help some babies settle, but volume and placement matter. The NHS doesn't set a specific decibel limit, but the Lullaby Trust and paediatric guidance advise keeping sound low, away from the cot, and not running all night.

Safe volume

Keep white noise at the level of a soft shower – about 50 decibels or lower. You should be able to talk over it easily. If you need to raise your voice, it's too loud. Place the device at least 2 metres from baby's head, never in the cot.

How long to use it

Use white noise to help baby fall asleep, then turn it off or set a timer for 30-60 minutes. Continuous all-night noise isn't recommended and can mask sounds you need to hear. The Lullaby Trust notes that constant loud noise may affect hearing development.

What to avoid

Avoid phone apps placed next to baby, max volume, or devices with loops that click. Avoid using white noise to cover an unsafe sleep environment – a clear cot and back sleeping remain more important than any sound. See Lullaby Trust for safer sleep essentials.
