Baby Waking Every Hour? Checklist

Check these 5 things before you try a sleep training method.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

If your baby is waking every hour overnight, it's usually a basic need or environment issue, not a habit. Work through this NHS-aligned checklist for babies 0-12 months.

1. Temperature check

Is the room 16-20°C? Overheating is the top cause of frequent waking. Feel chest or back of neck – warm, not sweaty. See our TOG guide for what to wear.

2. Hunger and feeds

Under 6 months, most babies still need night feeds. Over 6 months, check daytime calories – waking can mean not enough milk/solids in the day, not a sleep problem.

3. Sleep environment

Clear cot, firm flat mattress, baby on back. Is white noise too loud or too close? Keep it under 50dB and 2m away – see white noise safety. Dark room, no cot bumpers or toys.

4. Overtired vs undertired

Overtired babies wake more. For 0-6 months, aim for 45-90 minute awake windows. For 6-12 months, 2-3 hours. Watch for yawning and eye-rubbing, not the clock alone.

5. Check health basics

Nappy rash, reflux, stuffy nose, or teething can fragment sleep. NHS advises: if baby is feeding poorly, has a fever, or is unusually sleepy, contact your GP or health visitor – don't push sleep training.

When to get help

If waking persists after fixing the above for 3-4 nights, speak to your health visitor. Keep following safe sleep 0-6 and 6-12 guidance at all times.
