Baby Waking Every Hour? NHS Checklist

Check these 5 things first – before you try sleep training.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad of two • Checked against NHS guidance • Last updated 17 April 2026

If your baby is waking every hour overnight, it's usually a basic need or environment issue, not a "bad habit". According to NHS guidance for babies 0-12 months, work through this checklist before considering any sleep training method.

For babies under 6 months, waking every 2-3 hours for feeds is developmentally normal. Waking every hour consistently for multiple nights warrants checking these basics first.

1. Temperature check – is baby too hot or cold?

NHS recommends keeping baby's room between 16-20°C. Overheating is one of the most common causes of frequent waking, and is also a risk factor identified in safe sleep guidance.

How to check: Feel baby's chest or back of neck – it should feel warm, not hot or sweaty. Hands and feet are normally cooler.

What we did: Our Belfast bedroom was 17°C in winter. We used a 2.5 TOG sleep bag with vest and sleepsuit. When we tried a blanket instead (before we knew better), our daughter woke every 45 minutes – she was kicking it off and getting cold.

Use a room thermometer at cot height, not a wall thermostat. See our TOG ratings guide for exactly what to wear at 16°C, 18°C, 20°C.

2. Hunger and daytime feeds

Under 6 months: Most babies still need 1-3 night feeds. Waking hourly may mean baby is not transferring enough milk during the day, or is going through a growth spurt.

Over 6 months: NHS guidance suggests babies can often sleep longer stretches if getting adequate calories in daytime. Check: are you offering milk before solids? Is baby actually swallowing solids or just playing?

We found our son woke hourly at 7 months when we dropped his bedtime bottle too early. Adding 120ml of milk at 6:30pm reduced wakings to 2-3 times per night within 3 days.

3. Sleep environment audit

NHS safe sleep guidance specifies:

  • Clear cot – no toys, bumpers, pillows, or loose bedding
  • Firm, flat, waterproof mattress
  • Baby on back for all sleeps
  • Dark room (blackout blinds help)
  • White noise under 50dB and at least 2 metres from cot – see our white noise safety guide

We use the Yogasleep Rohm on lowest setting, across the room. When we had it too close and too loud, it was actually causing more wakings.

4. Overtired vs undertired – timing is everything

According to NHS health visitor advice, overtired babies actually sleep worse, not better. They release cortisol which causes frequent waking.

0-3 months: 45-60 minute awake windows
3-6 months: 90 minutes to 2 hours
6-12 months: 2-3 hours

Watch for sleep cues (yawning, eye rubbing, staring) not the clock. We tracked wakings for 3 days and realised our 5-month-old was awake 2.5 hours before bed – way too long. Moving bedtime 30 minutes earlier cut hourly wakings by half.

5. Check health basics first

NHS advises contacting your GP or health visitor if baby is waking hourly AND has any of these:

  • Feeding poorly or refusing feeds
  • Fever over 38°C
  • Unusually sleepy or irritable
  • Persistent cough or breathing difficulty
  • Signs of ear infection (pulling ear, fever)

Common non-urgent causes: nappy rash (try barrier cream overnight), reflux (keep upright 20-30 mins after feed), stuffy nose (saline drops), teething (offer cold teether before bed). Do not start sleep training when baby is unwell.

What we tried that didn't work

Before fixing the basics, we tried: dummies (fell out hourly), rocking to sleep (woke on transfer), and "cry it out" at 4 months (made it worse). NHS guidance recommends establishing safe sleep environment and addressing physical needs before behavioural approaches.

When to get professional help

If you've worked through this checklist for 4-5 nights with no improvement, speak to your health visitor. Keep following safe sleep 0-6 months and 6-12 months guidance at all times.

Persistent hourly waking beyond 6 months, especially with poor weight gain, warrants medical review to rule out reflux, allergies, or other issues.

Our turning point

For us, it was temperature + timing. We were using a 1.0 TOG bag in a 17°C room (too cold), and keeping our 6-month-old up until 8pm (overtired). Fixing both – switching to 2.5 TOG and 7pm bedtime – reduced wakings from hourly to 2-3 times per night within a week. Not perfect, but manageable.
