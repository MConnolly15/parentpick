TOG Ratings Made Simple

What to put baby in for UK room temperatures, without the guessing.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

TOG is just a measure of warmth. Higher TOG = warmer. The NHS says keep baby's room 16-20°C, but UK houses in winter are often 16-18°C, and summer can hit 24°C. Getting TOG wrong is the #1 reason babies wake sweaty or cold.

Quick TOG chart for UK homes

What we use in Belfast

Our house is 17-18°C in winter. We use a 2.5 TOG sleep bag with a long-sleeve vest and sleepsuit. In summer heatwaves (22-23°C), we switch to 1.0 TOG with just a vest. One 2.5 TOG and one 1.0 TOG covers the whole year – you don't need five.

How to check if TOG is right

Feel baby's chest or back of neck – not hands or feet. Warm = perfect. Sweaty or damp = too hot, remove a layer or drop TOG. Cool to touch = too cold, add a layer. Don't go by your own temperature – you have a duvet, baby doesn't.

TOG and safe sleep rules

TOG doesn't replace the safe sleep checklist. Still: back to sleep, clear cot, feet to foot, no hats indoors, no extra blankets over sleep bag. Never use adult duvet or weighted sleep bag under 12 months.

Common mistakes

  • Using 2.5 TOG in summer: Causes overheating, a SIDS risk. Switch to 1.0 or 0.5 above 21°C.
  • Layering blankets over sleep bag: Don't. Choose the right TOG instead.
  • Guessing room temp: £8 room thermometer is more accurate than your thermostat downstairs. Baby's room is often 2°C colder.
  • Buying 3.5 TOG for UK winter: Most UK homes with heating don't need it. 2.5 TOG + proper clothes is enough down to 16°C.

What about newborns?

0-3 months follow the same chart, but newborns lose heat faster. Keep room at 18-20°C if possible, use 2.5 TOG with vest and sleepsuit. Don't swaddle in a sleep bag – choose one or the other, and stop swaddling at first signs of rolling.

Summer heatwave guidance

Above 24°C, NHS advises: 0.2-0.5 TOG or just nappy, keep room cool with curtains closed in day, fan on low (not pointing at baby), offer extra feeds. Check for sweating every 2 hours.

Do I need different TOGs for naps?

Use same TOG as night if room temperature is similar. Daytime rooms are often warmer – check with thermometer, you may need to drop to 1.0 TOG for daytime naps in summer.

Buying guide – what to look for

Look for: UK safety standard BS EN 16781, shoulder poppers for easy changes, no hood, correct size (not too big). Two-way zip helps night changes. You need 2 bags minimum – one to wear, one in wash.
