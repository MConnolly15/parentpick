WHAT TO WEAR

TOG Ratings Made Simple

Stop guessing what baby should wear at 16-20°C.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

TOG measures warmth. For UK homes, most parents need just two sleep bags: a 2.5 TOG for winter and 1.0 TOG for spring/autumn, plus a 0.5 TOG or just a vest for hot nights. The NHS recommends keeping baby's room 16-20°C.

Quick TOG chart for 16-20°C

16-17°C: 2.5 TOG sleep bag + long-sleeve vest and sleepsuit
18-19°C: 2.5 TOG + long-sleeve vest, or 1.0 TOG + sleepsuit
20-21°C: 1.0 TOG + short-sleeve vest and sleepsuit, or just sleepsuit
22°C+: 0.5 TOG or just a vest. Never use a hat indoors for sleep.

How to check

Feel baby's chest or back of neck – it should be warm, not hot or sweaty. Hands and feet are usually cooler, don't use them to judge. Use a room thermometer, not your own feeling of temperature.

Common mistakes

Adding blankets over a sleep bag, using adult duvet TOG ratings, or overdressing when baby has a cold. If baby is unwell, check temperature more often but keep the same 16-20°C room guidance from the NHS.
