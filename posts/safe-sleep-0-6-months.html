Safe Sleep Checklist: 0-6 Months

NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance, explained by a Belfast dad.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

The first six months are the highest risk period for Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS) in the UK. The NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance hasn't changed in years because it works: back to sleep, clear cot, same room. This is the checklist we used for both our boys.

The 6 non-negotiables

1. Always on their back – for every sleep, day and night. Not side, not tummy. If they roll themselves later, that's fine, but start them on their back.

2. Feet to foot of cot – baby's feet at the bottom of the Moses basket or cot, so they can't wriggle under blankets.

3. Clear cot – mattress only. No pillows, duvets, bumpers, toys, pods, nests, or loose blankets. A fitted sheet only.

4. Same room, separate sleep space – NHS advises room-sharing for the first 6 months, day and night. Not in your bed, not on sofa – in their own cot or Moses basket next to you.

5. No smoking – around baby, in the house, or in the car. Smoking increases SIDS risk significantly.

6. 16-20°C room – not too hot, not too cold. Use a room thermometer, not your hand.

What about blankets vs sleep bags?

NHS says both are safe if used correctly, but sleep bags are easier. If using blankets: one thin cellular blanket, tucked in firmly below shoulders, feet to foot. No duvets, quilts, or weighted blankets under 12 months. We switched to a 2.5 TOG sleep bag at 3 weeks and never looked back – see our TOG guide for UK temperatures.

Feet to foot – why it matters

Babies wriggle. Feet to foot stops them moving down under covers and overheating. Place baby with feet touching the foot of the cot, blanket tucked in at sides and bottom only, reaching up to chest, arms free.

Room-sharing, not bed-sharing

The Lullaby Trust is clear: the safest place is a separate cot in your room for 6 months. Bed-sharing increases risk, especially if you smoke, drank alcohol, took medication that makes you sleepy, or baby was premature/low birth weight. Sofas and armchairs are never safe for sleep – risk is 50x higher.

If you feed to sleep

It's normal. Feed in a chair where you can't fall asleep. If you think you might doze, put baby back in cot first. See our feeding to sleep guide.

Temperature and overheating

Overheating is a SIDS risk factor. Keep room 16-20°C. Dress baby in one more layer than you. Check chest or back of neck – should be warm, not hot or sweaty. Hands and feet are normally cool. Don't use hats indoors, don't use hot water bottles or electric blankets.

Daytime naps – same rules

Every sleep counts. Use the same clear cot for naps, in the same room as you. Car seats and bouncers are for travel, not sleep – move baby to flat surface as soon as possible.

What not to buy

NHS and Lullaby Trust advise against: cot bumpers, sleep positioners, wedges, pods, nests, pillows, duvets before 12 months, and any product claiming to prevent SIDS. None are proven and many increase risk.

Common questions from new parents

What if baby hates back sleeping?

Keep trying. Back is safest. Swaddling can help in early weeks but stop once signs of rolling – usually 8-12 weeks. Then use sleep bag.

Can baby sleep on my chest?

Only when you're awake and alert. Never fall asleep with baby on chest on sofa or in bed.

Do I need a breathing monitor?

NHS does not recommend them. They haven't been shown to prevent SIDS and can cause false alarms.

After 6 months

Risk drops significantly after 6 months. You can move baby to their own room if you want, and rolling to tummy is fine. Keep the clear cot rules until 12 months. See safe sleep 6-12 months.
