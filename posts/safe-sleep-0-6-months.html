SAFE SLEEP

Safe Sleep Checklist: 0-6 Months (NHS-Aligned)

Six simple, evidence-based steps to reduce SIDS risk, backed by the NHS and Lullaby Trust.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Reviewed against NHS guidance • Last updated 15 April 2026

The NHS safe sleep checklist for babies 0-6 months has six steps: 1) always place baby on their back, 2) keep the cot completely clear, 3) use a firm flat waterproof mattress, 4) keep room temperature 16-20°C, 5) sleep in the same room for six months, 6) keep baby smoke-free. This follows Lullaby Trust guidance.

1. Back to sleep, every time

Always place your baby on their back to sleep, for every nap day and night. This is the safest position and significantly reduces the risk of SIDS. Once they can roll both ways independently, you don’t need to turn them back. See NHS guidance.

2. Clear cot = safe cot

Keep the cot or Moses basket completely clear. No pillows, duvets, cot bumpers, toys, or loose blankets. Use a firm, flat, waterproof mattress with a fitted sheet only, and choose a well-fitted baby sleep bag instead of blankets. The Lullaby Trust is clear: a clear cot is a safe cot.

3. Room temperature 16-20°C

Keep your baby’s room at 16–20°C – use a room thermometer, not guesswork. Dress baby in light layers and check their chest or back of neck, not hands or feet, to gauge temperature. Overheating increases risk, so never use hats indoors for sleep.

4. Same room, separate bed

Your baby should sleep in the same room as you, day and night, for at least the first six months. Use a separate cot or Moses basket next to your bed – never on a sofa or armchair. Bed-sharing is not recommended as it increases SIDS risk, per NHS advice.

5. What to avoid

Avoid smoking in pregnancy and keep your baby in a smoke-free environment – it’s a major risk factor. Never fall asleep with baby after drinking alcohol, taking drugs, or medication that makes you drowsy. Do not use pods, nests, wedges, weighted blankets or sleep positioners – they are not NHS-approved and go against Lullaby Trust guidance.
