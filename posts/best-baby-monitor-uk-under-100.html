What matters under £100

  • DECT not WiFi: Private digital signal. No internet needed, no hacking risk. This is what we use.
  • Range in UK houses: Thick walls kill signal. Look for 300m claimed (real world 20-30m through walls).
  • Battery: 8+ hours for parent unit overnight.
  • Video vs audio: For the first year, audio tells you everything. Video is nice-to-have, drains battery, and cheap video under £100 has poor night vision.

Video under £100: our take

We borrowed a Motorola video monitor (£70-90 range) for a week when family visited. It's non-WiFi video with a 5" screen. Picture was clear enough to see breathing at night, battery lasted about 6-7 hours.

We went back to audio. The screen light woke us up, and we found we didn't need to see her – hearing was enough. If you really want video and can't stretch past £100, look at the Motorola VM range. I would avoid the £40-60 Amazon video monitors – reviews mention poor battery and signal drops, and we returned a cheap WiFi one ourselves.

Do you need a monitor?

Probably not for the first 6 months. NHS and Lullaby Trust advise baby sleeps in the same room as you for SIDS risk reduction. When our daughter was in a next-to-me crib, we didn't use a monitor at all – we could hear her.

We started using ours at month 7 when she moved to her own room. Many parents buy monitors they never use.

Setup tips from our house

Place the baby unit at least 1 metre from the cot (safe sleep guidance). Keep it away from your WiFi router – ours caused a buzz on a cheap analogue monitor we tried, not on DECT. Test the range in daytime before first night use.
