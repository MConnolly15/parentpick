Best Baby Monitor UK Under £100 (2026)

You don't need £200 WiFi cameras. These three do the job.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Tested in 2-bed terrace • Prices checked April 2026

We bought a £179 WiFi monitor first. It disconnected every night, app drained phone battery, and we worried about hacking. Switched to a £49 DECT audio monitor and slept better. NHS doesn't recommend monitors for SIDS prevention – they're for parental reassurance only. Don't let marketing convince you otherwise.

What matters under £100

  • DECT not WiFi: Private, no internet, works in power cut (battery backup). WiFi under £100 is unreliable and insecure.
  • Range: UK houses with thick walls need 300m+ claimed range (real world 30-50m)
  • Battery life: 8+ hours minimum for parent unit
  • No video needed: Audio tells you everything for first 6 months. Video is nice-to-have, not essential.

Our pick: BT Audio 450 (£40)

We've used this for 14 months in Belfast terrace with solid walls. Range reaches garden (about 25m through 2 walls). Battery lasts 12 hours, has night light on baby unit, two-way talkback, and temperature alert. No app, no WiFi, just works. Sound is crystal clear – you hear every snuffle without the white noise hiss of cheap models. £39.99 at Argos, often £35 on offer.

Best cheap: VTech BM1000 (£30)

If money is tight, this is the one. Basic DECT audio, 460m range claimed, belt clip, vibrate alert. No frills, but we tested a friend's and it worked fine through a 3-bed semi. Battery 8 hours. At £29.99 it's the best value in UK right now. Downsides: no temperature sensor, slightly more background hiss than BT.

Best video under £100: Motorola Comfort 75 (£90)

Only video monitor under £100 worth buying. 5" screen, remote pan/tilt, infrared night vision, two-way talk. Not WiFi – private 2.4GHz signal. Battery lasts 6-7 hours (video drains). We borrowed one for a week – picture is good enough to see breathing, not HD but fine. Range about 20m through walls. If you must have video and can't stretch to £150+, this is it. £89.99 at Currys April 2026.

What to avoid under £100

Any WiFi monitor claiming "1080p app control" for £50-70 – they use Chinese servers, drop connection, and have security flaws. Also avoid non-DECT analogue monitors – neighbours can hear you. Stick to DECT digital.

Do you actually need a monitor?

NHS and Lullaby Trust say room-sharing for first 6 months is safest, which means baby is next to you – you don't need a monitor. We didn't use ours until month 7 when we moved baby to own room. Many UK parents buy monitors and never use them. If budget is tight, skip it for first 6 months.

Setup tips

Place baby unit 1m from cot, not in cot. Keep away from WiFi router to avoid interference. Test range before first night. Use rechargeable AA batteries in parent unit for backup. Don't rely on monitor for safe sleep – it doesn't prevent SIDS, see our safe sleep guide.

Where to buy cheapest

Argos often price matches Amazon. Currys has 21-day returns which is useful for testing range in your house. Avoid marketplace sellers – warranty issues. All three models have 2-year UK warranty.
