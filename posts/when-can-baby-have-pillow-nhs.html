When Can Baby Have a Pillow? NHS Guidance

Short answer: not until 12 months minimum. Here's why.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS and Lullaby Trust • Last updated 16 April 2026

NHS guidance: No pillows, duvets, or cot bumpers for babies under 12 months. The Lullaby Trust recommends waiting until at least 12 months, ideally 18-24 months.

We get asked this constantly in our NCT group – "my 8-month-old seems uncomfortable flat, can I give a small pillow?" The answer from every health visitor we've spoken to in Belfast is the same: no. Babies don't need pillows, and they increase suffocation risk.

Why pillows are dangerous under 12 months

  • Suffocation risk: Baby's head can sink into soft pillow, blocking airway. They lack neck strength to lift head clear until around 12 months.
  • Overheating: Pillows trap heat around head – overheating is a known SIDS risk factor.
  • SIDS risk: Lullaby Trust data shows soft bedding including pillows increases SIDS risk 2-3x in under-12s.
  • Spinal development: Babies' spines are designed to lie flat. Pillows can force unnatural neck angle.

What age is actually safe?

12 months: Minimum age NHS mentions, but most experts say wait longer.

18 months: When many UK parents introduce a very flat, infant pillow (not adult pillow).

24 months+: Ideal age. Toddler has good head control, can move pillow away if uncomfortable, and is in cot bed or bed.

We waited until 2 years 3 months when we moved to toddler bed. Used a flat 25x35cm toddler pillow, not a standard pillow.

But my baby sleeps better propped up?

If baby has reflux, NHS advises talking to GP – don't prop with pillows. Safe alternatives: hold upright for 20-30 minutes after feed, slightly elevate head of cot by placing books under cot legs (not mattress), ensure correct TOG so not overheating. Never use sleep positioners or wedges sold online – they're not approved and increase risk.

What shops sell "baby pillows" for 0-6 months?

They exist, marketed as "breathable" or "anti-flat head". Lullaby Trust explicitly advises against them. Flat head (plagiocephaly) is cosmetic and resolves with tummy time, not pillows. These products are not safety tested to UK standards for under-12s and are not recommended by NHS.

What to use instead

Nothing. Baby sleeps flat on firm, waterproof mattress, in sleep bag. That's it. No pillow, no duvet, no blanket, no toys. See our 0-6 month safe sleep checklist and 6-12 month guide.

If worried about comfort: babies are comfortable flat. They've slept that way for 9 months in womb. Adult need for pillows doesn't apply.

When you do introduce a pillow

At 18-24 months: choose a toddler pillow (thin, firm, 25x35cm), not adult pillow. Use pillowcase only, no duvet until 18 months minimum. Keep cot/bed clear of other soft toys. Introduce at same time as moving to cot bed to avoid multiple changes.
