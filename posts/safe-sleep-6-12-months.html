Safe Sleep 6-12 Months

What changes after 6 months, and what stays the same.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

After 6 months, SIDS risk drops significantly, but safe sleep still matters. The biggest changes: baby can roll, you can consider their own room, and sleep associations become the main challenge – not safety basics.

What stays the same

  • Back to sleep to start – always place on back
  • Clear cot – no pillows, duvets, bumpers, toys until 12 months
  • Flat, firm mattress, waterproof cover
  • Room temperature 16-20°C
  • No smoking around baby

Rolling to tummy – what to do

Once baby can roll both ways confidently (usually 5-7 months), you don't need to flip them back. NHS and Lullaby Trust say: place on back, but let them find their own position. Ensure cot is clear so they can breathe freely. Stop swaddling immediately when rolling starts – switch to sleep bag.

Moving to own room

NHS recommends room-sharing for first 6 months. After 6 months, you can move baby to their own room if you want. Many UK parents wait until 9-12 months. If you move: use a baby monitor, keep same safe sleep setup, keep door open or ajar for airflow.

Pillows and duvets – wait until 12 months

Despite what shops sell, NHS advises no pillow or duvet until at least 12 months, ideally 18 months. Use a sleep bag with appropriate TOG instead. Pillows increase suffocation risk and aren't needed for spinal development.

Cot bumpers and positioners

Still not recommended 6-12 months. Bumpers are a suffocation and strangulation risk, and don't prevent injuries. If baby gets limbs stuck, it's uncomfortable but not dangerous – they'll learn to move. Mesh "breathable" bumpers are also not recommended by Lullaby Trust.

Teething and sleep regressions

6-12 months brings more waking, but it's rarely unsafe sleep – it's developmental. Check: temperature, hunger, teething pain, separation anxiety. Keep the safe sleep environment consistent even when sleep is messy.

From cot to cot bed

Most babies stay in cot until 18-24 months or until climbing out. At 6-12 months, keep cot on lowest base setting once baby can pull to stand. No need to transition early.

What changes for parents

You can relax slightly, but not completely. The 6-month mark is when many parents drop vigilance – keep the clear cot rule. If you're reducing night feeds, do it safely without introducing loose bedding.
