Safe Sleep Checklist: 6-12 Months

What changes after 6 months, and what stays the same.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

After 6 months, SIDS risk drops but safe sleep still matters. The NHS says baby can move to their own room after 6 months if you choose, and rolling is normal.

1. Rolling is fine

Put baby down on their back, but if they roll to tummy, you don't need to flip them back. Don't use positioners or wedges – they're not NHS-approved.

2. Own room – your choice

NHS advises room-sharing for the first 6 months. After that, moving to their own cot in their own room is safe. Keep the same clear-cot rules from 0-6 months.

3. Keep the cot clear

No pillows, duvets, bumpers, toys, or cot nests. Use a sleep bag instead of blankets. Check TOG ratings for 16-20°C.

4. Stop swaddling

Once baby shows signs of rolling (usually 3-4 months), stop swaddling. Move to a sleep bag with arms free.

5. Same temperature rules

16-20°C still applies. Don't over-dress because they're older. Feel chest or back of neck, not hands.

What about white noise and feeding?

Both are still fine if used safely – see white noise and feeding to sleep.
