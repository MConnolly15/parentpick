Safe Sleep 6-12 Months: NHS Guidance UK 2026

By ParentPick Editorial Team | Medically reviewed against NHS and Lullaby Trust sources | Updated 20 April 2026

According to NHS Start for Life, SIDS risk reduces significantly after six months, but safer sleep practices remain important. The Lullaby Trust notes this is the period when babies become mobile, making a clear cot even more critical.

This article summarises what changes after six months, and what UK public health guidance states should stay the same until 12 months.

What NHS says stays the same 6-12 months

According to NHS guidance, the following measures continue to apply:

  • Back to sleep to start. Public health guidance states babies should be placed on their back for every sleep. The Lullaby Trust advises you do not need to reposition them if they roll.
  • Clear cot until 12 months. NHS advises no pillows, duvets, bumpers, toys, or loose blankets. The Lullaby Trust specifically recommends against introducing any loose bedding before 12 months. For full detail on blankets, see when can baby sleep with a blanket.
  • Flat, firm mattress. Public health guidance states the mattress should remain firm, flat, and waterproof with a fitted sheet only.
  • Room temperature 16-20°C. According to NHS, this remains the ideal range. See room temperature guidance.
  • Smoke-free. NHS guidance states this remains critical throughout the first year.

Rolling to tummy – NHS guidance

According to the Lullaby Trust, once a baby can roll both ways confidently (usually between 5-7 months), parents should continue placing them on their back to start each sleep, but do not need to turn them back if they roll onto their tummy. Public health guidance states this is because babies who can roll have developed the strength to protect their airway.

NHS advises that swaddling must stop immediately at the first signs of rolling, typically 8-12 weeks. The Lullaby Trust recommends transitioning to a baby sleep bag with appropriate TOG rating instead. Many parents choose an arms-free sleep bag like the Tommee Tippee Grobag once rolling starts. Moving to own room after 6 months

According to NHS Start for Life, room-sharing is recommended for the first six months for all sleeps. After six months, public health guidance states parents may choose to move baby to their own room.

The Lullaby Trust notes many UK families wait until 9-12 months. NHS advises if moving to own room: maintain the same clear cot setup, use a baby monitor, keep the door open or ajar for airflow, and maintain room temperature at 16-20°C.

Pillows and duvets – why NHS says wait until 12 months

According to NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance, pillows and duvets are not recommended for babies under 12 months. Public health guidance states they increase suffocation and overheating risk and are not needed for spinal development.

The Lullaby Trust recommends continuing with a sleep bag until at least 12 months, with many families continuing to 18-24 months. NHS advises that from 12 months, a small light blanket may be introduced if parents wish, though sleep bags remain the safer option. See when can baby have a pillow and blanket guidance.

Cot bumpers and positioners 6-12 months

According to the Lullaby Trust, cot bumpers remain not recommended for babies 6-12 months, including mesh "breathable" versions. Public health guidance states they pose suffocation, strangulation and overheating risks.

NHS advises that if baby gets limbs stuck between cot bars, this is uncomfortable but not dangerous, and babies quickly learn to move. The Lullaby Trust notes bumpers do not prevent injuries and were designed for drafty homes, not modern nurseries.

Teething, regressions and safe sleep

According to NHS, 6-12 months often brings more night waking due to teething, separation anxiety, and developmental leaps. The Lullaby Trust advises maintaining the safe sleep environment consistently, even during regressions.

Public health guidance states to check temperature, hunger, and comfort, but not to introduce loose bedding, pillows or duvets as a solution to waking. If you are reducing night feeds, NHS advises doing so without introducing unsafe sleep practices.

From cot to cot bed

According to NHS, most babies remain in a cot until 18-24 months or until they begin climbing out. The Lullaby Trust recommends keeping the cot on the lowest base setting once baby can pull to stand, usually around 8-10 months. Public health guidance states there is no need to transition to a bed at 6-12 months.

In summary, according to NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance, the 6-12 month period sees reduced SIDS risk but requires continued vigilance: place baby on their back to start, maintain a clear cot with no pillows or duvets until 12 months, keep room temperature 16-20°C, and transition from swaddle to sleep bag once rolling begins. Parents considering blankets or pillows should refer to NHS guidance which recommends waiting until at least 12 months. For the complete foundation, see safe sleep 0-6 months.
