Room Temperature for Baby: NHS 16-20°C Guide

How to check it right, and what to do when your UK house doesn't cooperate.

Written by Mark, Belfast dad • Checked against NHS and Lullaby Trust guidance • Last updated 16 April 2026

The NHS says 16-20°C is the safest range for baby's room. Not because babies need it cold – because overheating is a known SIDS risk factor. UK houses in winter sit at 16-18°C, in summer they hit 24°C+. Both are manageable if you dress baby correctly.

Why 16-20°C matters

Babies can't regulate temperature like adults. Too hot = increased SIDS risk, dehydration, restless sleep. Too cold = baby burns calories staying warm, wakes frequently. The 16-20°C window is the sweet spot identified by the Lullaby Trust after reviewing UK data.

How to check without a fancy gadget

Best method (£8): Digital room thermometer placed at cot height, away from radiator and window. Check it at bedtime and 2am for first week – you'll learn your house pattern.

No thermometer: Feel baby's chest or back of neck. Warm and dry = perfect. Sweaty, damp hair, flushed cheeks = too hot. Cool chest = too cold. Hands and feet are always cooler – ignore them.

Your comfort is misleading: You're under a duvet. Baby is in a sleep bag. If you're comfortable in a t-shirt, room is probably 20-22°C – too warm for baby in 2.5 TOG.

UK winter: when room is 16-17°C

Common in Victorian terraces and flats with heating off overnight. Don't use extra blankets. Use: 2.5 TOG sleep bag + vest + sleepsuit + socks. If below 16°C, heat the room – don't add layers. NHS says never use hot water bottles, electric blankets, or heat baby directly.

UK summer: when room is 22-24°C

Heatwaves are harder. Use: 1.0 TOG or 0.5 TOG, vest only or just nappy. Keep curtains closed all day, open windows at night, fan on low pointing away from cot. Offer extra feeds. Check baby every 2-3 hours for sweating.

Above 25°C: Strip to nappy, 0.2 TOG or none, keep room as cool as possible. This is when overheating risk is highest.

Signs of overheating

  • Sweaty neck, chest, or hair
  • Flushed red cheeks
  • Rapid breathing
  • Heat rash on chest or neck
  • Baby feels hot to touch

If you see these, remove a layer immediately, cool room, offer feed.

Signs baby is cold

  • Chest feels cool (not hands)
  • Pale skin, lethargic
  • Waking frequently and not settling

Add a layer or increase TOG, but check room isn't below 16°C first.

Do I need a specific baby thermometer?

No. Any digital room thermometer is fine. The Gro-Egg is popular in UK because it glows colour-coded, but a £8 ThermoPro from Amazon is just as accurate. Place it at cot level, not on a high shelf where heat rises.

Common UK mistakes

Heating room to 23°C because parents feel cold, then using 2.5 TOG – causes overheating. Using a duvet over sleep bag in winter. Putting cot against radiator or in direct sun. Relying on central heating thermostat downstairs – baby's room is usually 2-3°C colder.
